Biography paragraph on Mahatma Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, later known as Mahatma Gandhi,
was a pivotal figure in India's struggle for independence from British
. Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India , he became a lawyer and later a political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the Indian independence movement.
Gandhi's philosophy of satyagraha, or nonviolent resistance, inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the globe.
He is revered as the "Father of the Nation" in India and is globally recognized for his commitment to peace and social justice.

Movements by Mahatma Gandhi:

Champaran Satyagraha (1917):


This movement focused on the rights of farmers oppressed by British indigo planters in Bihar.

Kheda Satyagraha (1918):


Gandhi supported farmers in Gujarat who were facing hardships due to crop failure and high taxes.

Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922):


This was a nationwide movement to boycott British goods, institutions, and titles to pressure the British government.

Salt Satyagraha/Salt March (1930):


Aimed at challenging the British salt tax, this march to Dandi became a powerful symbol of resistance.

Quit India Movement (1942):


This movement demanded an immediate end to British rule and a complete withdrawal from India.
